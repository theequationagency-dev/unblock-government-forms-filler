# Our Mission

**Everyone deserves to participate. This project exists to make sure a blocked form
is never the reason someone gets shut out.**

---

## Why I built this

I am a **political candidate**, and I built Form Filler out of direct personal
experience. I found myself being blocked — unable to complete online forms, register
for events, and take part in processes that are supposed to be open to everyone. When
the ordinary way in stops working for you, it is isolating, and it is easy to feel
powerless.

I do not believe this is unique to me. I believe the same barriers fall on **founders,
small-business owners, organizers, and everyday residents** all over. Sometimes it's a
broken browser or a buggy website. Sometimes, I believe, it is something more
deliberate — because when individuals hold privileged, back-end access to the systems
we all depend on, that access can be misused to interfere with a person's ability to
participate in public and civic life.

This application is my **constructive answer** to that problem. Not anger — a tool.

## What this tool does about it

Form Filler cannot force a third party's website to accept you. What it *can* do is
make sure you are never stuck:

- **Capture your information once**, on your own device, and reuse it instantly.
- **Complete any registration form** — for a seminar, an event, a webinar, or a
  general/official process — cleanly and quickly.
- **Keep your own copy** as a printable PDF, so there is always a record you control.
- **Jump straight to the real sign-up page** with one tap when you're ready to submit.

Everything runs **offline, on your device**. Nothing you enter is sent to any server —
not to me, not to anyone. Your participation, your data, your control.

## Principles

1. **Access is a right.** No one should lose their place in public life because a form
   won't cooperate.
2. **Privacy is non-negotiable.** Your information stays on your device. Always.
3. **Free and open.** The code is public and MIT-licensed so anyone can use it, inspect
   it, trust it, and build on it.
4. **Constructive, not combative.** We respond to being blocked by building better
   tools, not by tearing anything down.

## What's next

This is the **first public release** — the foundation. I am going to keep building:
more tools that help people actually use websites and get through the things they are
being kept out of. A **second part is already planned.**

If this project helps you get un-blocked, that is the whole point. Use it, share it
with someone who needs it, and — if you can — [contribute](CONTRIBUTING.md).

**We don't just automate. We build access.**
